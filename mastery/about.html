Robert Greene

Author, Strategist, Student of Power and Human Nature

Biography

Robert Greene is an American author known for his books on strategy, power, and human nature. His work has influenced entrepreneurs, executives, and anyone seeking to understand how people operate.

Born in Los Angeles in 1959, Greene studied classical studies at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and the University of California, Berkeley.

Before becoming a full-time writer, Greene worked in various jobs—including as a magazine editor and Hollywood screenwriter—experiences that deepened his understanding of power dynamics and human behavior in real-world settings.

His breakthrough came with The 48 Laws of Power (1998), which has sold millions of copies and become a cult classic among entrepreneurs, executives, and anyone interested in understanding how power operates. He has since authored several influential works that blend history, psychology, and strategy.

Major Works

1998

The 48 Laws of Power

A distillation of 3,000 years of power dynamics into 48 laws, illustrated with historical examples. His seminal work.

2001

The Art of Seduction

An exploration of the psychology of attraction and influence through the lens of seduction.

2006

The 33 Strategies of War

Military strategy applied to everyday conflicts—business, relationships, and personal battles.

2009

The 50th Law

Co-authored with 50 Cent, examining fearlessness and the mindset of the hustler.

2012

Mastery

How to achieve mastery in any field by studying the paths of history's greatest masters.

2018

The Laws of Human Nature

His most introspective work—18 laws that explain why people do what they do, and how to understand yourself and others.

Why His Work Matters

Robert Greene's influence extends far beyond book sales. His work has been referenced by musicians, athletes, business leaders, and politicians. He has advised on strategy and consulted for various organizations.

What makes his writing enduring is its refusal to sugarcoat human nature. He presents the world as it often operates—with power plays, hidden motivations, and strategic considerations—while also advocating for self-awareness, empathy, and ethical application of these insights. The Laws of Human Nature in particular represents a maturation of his thinking, emphasizing understanding over manipulation.
